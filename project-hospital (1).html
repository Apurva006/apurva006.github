🔴
3 specialties carry 40%+ of Ireland's longest waits
Orthopaedics, Dermatology and Ophthalmology appear at the top of the backlog ranking every year from 2019 to 2024. This is a structural capacity problem — not a temporary post-COVID effect.
📈
No full recovery post-pandemic for critical specialties
While some areas improved in 2024, the three critical specialties remain 18–40% above pre-pandemic baselines. The data argues for targeted — not blanket — investment to get maximum impact per euro spent.
⚠️
Urology and ENT showing early warning signs
Both are trending upward before reaching critical level. Based on current trajectory, Tableau forecasting shows they are on track to join the critical tier within 2–3 years without intervention.
💡
Targeted intervention beats blanket spending
Excel cost modelling shows that directing resources at Orthopaedics and Dermatology specifically would address 40%+ of the 18-month+ backlog at significantly lower cost than system-wide expansion.
Policy Brief Recommendations
Framed for a hypothetical healthcare leadership audience · Evidence-based · Ranked by projected impact
01
NTPF Outsourcing — Dermatology
Routine procedures suitable for private outsourcing. International evidence suggests 30% wait reduction within 12 months at lower per-patient cost than hospital outpatient.
02
Physio-Led Triage — Orthopaedics
Redirect 35–40% of referrals to physiotherapy-led assessment. Reduces unnecessary consultant appointments without additional capital spend.
03
Community Dermatology Clinics
GPWSI model in primary care — evidence from NHS pilots shows 25% outpatient pressure reduction. Lower cost per patient than hospital setting.
04
Early Warning System
Monthly NTPF threshold alerts for Urology and ENT — triggers capacity planning review before lists reach critical and irreversible levels.
