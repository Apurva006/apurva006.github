The Full Business Analysis Lifecycle What BAs actually do
1. Requirements Elicited (from HR Director)
  • Identify which departments and roles have the highest attrition
  • Determine whether compensation, workload or management is the primary driver
  • Segment findings by tenure, age and job level to find actionable patterns
  • Deliver recommendations the HR Director can present to the board with ROI evidence
2. BRD Documented
  • Business Objective: Reduce attrition from 16.1% to under 11% within 12 months
  • Success Metric: Attrition rate below 11% by Q4 · Cost saving target: €1.8M
  • Stakeholders: HR Director · Finance (cost model) · Line Managers (operational impact)
  • Constraints: Analysis in Excel + Tableau only. No new systems. Low implementation cost.
3. Analysis Performed
  • Excel pivot tables by department, tenure band, pay grade and overtime hours
  • VLOOKUP to cross-reference manager satisfaction scores with team attrition rates
  • Tableau dashboard with manager-level drill-down and department filtering
  • Root cause analysis: overtime hours vs attrition — strongest correlation found
4. Stakeholder Output Delivered
  • 2-page executive summary for HR Director in non-technical language
  • Tableau dashboard for HR team day-to-day operational monitoring
  • Excel cost model for Finance to run scenario analysis on each recommendation
  • 4 recommendations ranked by projected ROI — ready for board presentation

🚨
Overtime is the #1 attrition predictor
Employees doing frequent overtime churn at 30.5% vs 10.4% for those who don't. This single variable predicts attrition better than pay, age or department — and it's actionable immediately through workload redistribution.
👶
Entry-level staff leave at 3× the rate of seniors
Level 1–2 employees show 28% attrition vs 9% at Level 4–5. Excel tenure analysis shows the highest risk window is months 6–18 — when initial enthusiasm fades but before the employee feels invested. A structured development programme in this window has high ROI.
💼
Sales attrition costs €828K annually
Sales has the highest cost impact of any department. Tableau drill-down shows Sales employees averaging 14+ overtime hours per week — nearly double the company average. Workload redistribution and 2 additional headcount would cost ~€120K but recover an estimated €480K in reduced attrition.
📋
Manager scores predict team attrition
VLOOKUP cross-referencing manager satisfaction scores with team attrition revealed a clear pattern: all 5 teams with attrition above 20% had manager scores below 6/10. Manager effectiveness is a leading indicator — not a lagging one.
Strategic Recommendations — Board-Ready
Ranked by projected ROI · Delivered with Excel cost model for Finance team
01
Sales Workload Cap + 2 FTE
Cap overtime at 10hrs/month. Add 2 FTE to redistribute load. Cost: €120K. Projected saving: €480K. Payback: 3 months.
02
Junior Development Programme
12-month structured learning + mentorship for Level 1–2 staff. Targets attrition 28% → 15%. Projected saving: €720K annually at current headcount.
03
Manager Effectiveness Reviews
Quarterly 360-degree feedback. Managers scoring below 6/10 receive coaching. All 5 high-attrition teams have low manager scores — this is the fastest win.
04
Structured Exit Interview Process
Standardised exit interviews feeding into a quarterly HR dashboard. Currently no systematic capture. This is a data blind spot that prevents future analysis from being this accurate.
